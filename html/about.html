The Turning Point

How Shravasti Captured My Heart

1

The First Encounter

I first heard about Shravasti while researching Buddhist pilgrimage sites. Unlike the more famous Bodh Gaya or Sarnath, Shravasti seemed quieter, less touristy, almost forgotten by mainstream travel circuits. That intrigued me. I've always been drawn to places that don't shout for attention but whisper their stories to those willing to listen.

2

The Journey Begins

Reaching Shravasti required determination‚Äîmultiple trains, bumpy bus rides, and navigating rural roads. But every challenge melted away the moment I stepped into Jetavana. The air felt different here, charged with centuries of meditation and prayer. I could almost hear the echoes of ancient chants carried on the wind.

3

The Transformation

What was supposed to be a two-day visit turned into two weeks. I found myself returning to Jetavana every morning, sitting under the Bodhi tree, watching pilgrims from around the world bring their hopes and prayers. I met an elderly Thai monk who taught me meditation techniques, a group of Japanese pilgrims who shared their lunch, and a local caretaker who told me stories passed down through generations.

4

The Revelation

One evening, as I watched the sunset paint the ancient stupas in golden hues, I had an epiphany. This place needed more than casual visits‚Äîit deserved to be understood, appreciated, and shared with others who seek meaningful travel experiences. That's when I decided to create this blog, not as a tourist guide, but as a love letter to Shravasti and everything it represents.
